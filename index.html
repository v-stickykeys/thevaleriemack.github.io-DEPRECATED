Clone Combat

Clone Combat is a game that my team and I created for our Analytics-Driven Game Design class. I came up with the concept for this puzzle platformer where the player has to defeat clones who mirror each of the player's inputs in real time. We successfully executed our ideas, creating three iterative versions that improved over time.

My primary roles were user interface and user experience designer.

Our data analysis showed that our greatest design challenge was learnability, so I created comprehensive tutorial levels to increase player engagement.

Clone Combat was one of the most successful games produced from our class. It was featured on Newgrounds and received a 3.5 / 5 rating and over 20,000 plays in the first week. With a tougher audience on Kongregate, we received a 3.1 / 5 star rating.

Mechanical Dreams

I did not have a formal internship this past summer, so I had to create one from scratch. Using my deveoping interest in werables, I turned to the watchmaking industry to learn more about the history of mechanical timepieces and where they are headed as technological capabilites develop further.

After networking at a wearables roundtable hosted at Aaron Faber gallery, I became the apprentice to a Manhattan watchmaker with 30+ years of experience at Tourneau, Rolex, and Tiffany & Co.

Throughout the summer, I used the knowledge I was gaining from my apprenticeship to design an educational watchmaking game for Art of Horology, the only watchmaking school in Manhattan. I was responsible for overseeing the project, and designing the gameplay and the user interface.

gix science

Gix is a startup that came out of Cornell's ELab program. I was asked to be the designer for their web application platform, aimed at transforming the way we do science.

The web app is a cloud-based data collection, analysis and storage platform where users can share and display content using iPython notebooks. It is similar to github, as it allows researchers a form of version control that can be easily examined, replicated, and experimented with by others.

Gix also provides a unique way for researchers to be recognized for the work they have contributed to by keeping track of when and where their publications have been cited.
